<p><p>The dental prosthetic and implant consumables market includes various types of implants, notably endosteal and subperiosteal implants. Endosteal implants are placed directly into the jawbone, providing a stable foundation for artificial teeth. They are the most common type and typically made of titanium. Subperiosteal implants, on the other hand, sit on top of the jawbone but beneath the gum tissue, making them suitable for patients with insufficient bone height. Both types play crucial roles in restoring dental functionality and aesthetics.</p></p>
