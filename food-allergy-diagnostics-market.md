<p><p>The food allergy diagnostics market includes various testing methods, notably Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) and Enzyme-linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA). PCR is a molecular technique that detects specific allergens by amplifying their DNA, allowing for precise identification. ELISA, on the other hand, is an immunological assay used to measure the presence of allergen-specific antibodies in blood samples, providing insights into sensitization levels. Both methods play critical roles in accurately diagnosing food allergies and guiding management strategies.</p></p>
