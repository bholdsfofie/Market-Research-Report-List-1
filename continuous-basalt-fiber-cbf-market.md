<p><p>Continuous Basalt Fiber (CBF) is a material derived from basalt rock, offering high strength and thermal resistance for various applications. The CBF market can be segmented into three main production methods: Centrifugal-Blowing, which uses high-speed spinning to create fine fibers; Centrifugal-Multiroll, which employs multiple rotating rolls for increased fiber production; and Die-Blowing, where molten basalt is extruded through a die to form continuous strands. Each method has unique advantages, catering to different industrial requirements.</p></p>
