<p><p>Ordinary life insurance encompasses various market types. Non-participating whole life policies provide fixed premiums and guaranteed death benefits without dividends, offering predictable costs. Participating whole life policies allow policyholders to receive dividends, which are derived from the insurer’s profits, providing potential cash value growth. Additionally, other markets may include term life insurance, which offers coverage for a specified period without cash value, or universal life insurance, which combines flexibility with a cash value component, responding to changing needs.</p></p>
