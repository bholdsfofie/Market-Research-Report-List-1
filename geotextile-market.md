<p><ul><li>Synthetic</li><li>Natural</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The geotextile market is primarily divided into synthetic and natural types. Synthetic geotextiles, made from polymers like polyethylene and polypropylene, are known for their durability and resistance to environmental factors, making them suitable for a wide range of engineering applications. In contrast, natural geotextiles, derived from organic materials such as jute, coir, and hemp, offer biodegradable solutions and are often used in erosion control and landscaping. Both types play crucial roles in soil stabilization, drainage, and the management of civil engineering projects.</p></p>
