<p><p>The High Temperature Grease and Lubricants market is categorized into two main types: soap thickener and non-soap thickener grease. Soap thickener greases, commonly made from lithium, calcium, or aluminum soaps, are known for their stability and water resistance, making them suitable for high-temperature applications. Non-soap thickener greases, such as clay or polyurea-based, offer superior heat resistance and performance in extreme conditions. Both types are essential for ensuring reliable lubrication in heavy machinery and automotive industries where temperatures exceed typical operational ranges.</p></p>
