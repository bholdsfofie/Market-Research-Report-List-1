<p><p>Lithium-ion batteries in the marine market feature several types, each with unique properties. Lithium Nickel Manganese Cobalt (NMC) offers high energy density and stability, while Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP) is known for safety and long cycle life. Lithium Cobalt Oxide (LCO) provides excellent energy density but has thermal stability concerns. Lithium Titanate Oxide (LTO) excels in fast charging and longevity. Lithium Manganese Oxide (LMO) balances performance and safety, whereas Lithium Nickel Cobalt Aluminum Oxide (NCA) delivers high energy density and thermal stability, making it suitable for marine applications.</p></p>
