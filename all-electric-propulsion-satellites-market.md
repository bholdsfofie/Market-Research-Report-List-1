<p><p>The all-electric propulsion satellite market comprises three key categories based on orbital altitude: Low Earth Orbit (LEO), Medium Earth Orbit (MEO), and Geosynchronous Earth Orbit (GEO). LEO satellites typically serve applications such as Earth observation and telecommunications due to their proximity to Earth. MEO satellites, often used for navigation and certain communications, provide a balance between coverage and latency. GEO satellites, positioned high above the equator, offer persistent coverage and are primarily used for TV broadcasting, weather monitoring, and long-range communication.</p></p>
