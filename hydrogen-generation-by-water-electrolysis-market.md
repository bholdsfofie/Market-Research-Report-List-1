<p><ul><li>Alkaline Water Electrolysis</li><li>Proton Exchange membrane (PEM) Electrolysis</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The hydrogen generation by water electrolysis market includes two primary types: Alkaline Water Electrolysis and Proton Exchange Membrane (PEM) Electrolysis. Alkaline Water Electrolysis employs a liquid electrolyte, typically potassium hydroxide, and is known for its cost-effectiveness and durability, making it suitable for large-scale applications. In contrast, PEM Electrolysis utilizes a solid polymer electrolyte, offering higher efficiency and faster response times, ideal for dynamic environments and smaller-scale operations. Both technologies play key roles in advancing clean hydrogen production.</p></p>
