<p><p>The cosmetic fragrance market is categorized into two primary types: pure natural extraction and chemical extraction. Pure natural extraction involves obtaining scents directly from natural sources, like flowers, fruits, and spices, often using methods like steam distillation or cold pressing. In contrast, chemical extraction entails synthesizing fragrances through petrochemical processes, resulting in more consistent and varied scent profiles. Both types cater to consumer preferences, with natural extracts appealing to those seeking organic, sustainable products, while chemical scents offer affordability and broad availability.</p></p>
