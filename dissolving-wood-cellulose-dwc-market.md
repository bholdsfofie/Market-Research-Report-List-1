<p><p>Dissolving Wood Cellulose (DWC) is a refined form of cellulose derived from wood pulp, primarily used in producing viscose and other fibers. The market for DWC includes two main types: Alkali Wood Pulp or Sulfate Wood Pulp, which involves the kraft process for stronger, higher-quality fibers; and Wood Pulp of Sulfite, which uses a sulfite process, yielding softer, less durable cellulose. Both types cater to textiles, food, and pharmaceutical industries, driving demand based on quality and applications.</p></p>
